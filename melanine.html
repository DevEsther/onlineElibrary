Despite her reluctance, Jemma convinces Angelica to take up the offer by informing her that it would be a great opportunity to make a name for herself in the industry. Angelica reluctantly agrees, and soon, she finds herself in the luxurious apartment of Marco Marcolini. Angelica is mesmerized by the grandeur of the place and the enigma of Marco. However, Marco is not impressed with Angelica's casual attire, and he orders her to change into something more glamorous for the shoot. As Angelica goes to the bathroom to change, she realizes that Marco's apartment is filled with diamonds, and she wonders what kind of man would live surrounded by such opulence. Angelica emerges from the bathroom in a stunning red dress, and Marco is impressed with her transformation. He then proceeds to direct the photo shoot, and Angelica is surprised to find that she is enjoying herself. She finds herself attracted to Marco's rugged good looks and charm. After the photo shoot, Marco asks Angelica to join him for dinner. Angelica agrees, and they head out to a fancy restaurant. During dinner, Marco reveals that he needs a wife to inherit his family business and continue his legacy. He proposes to Angelica, offering her a marriage of convenience, and in return, he promises to help her achieve her dreams of becoming a successful model. Angelica is stunned by the proposal and hesitant to accept it, but Marco manages to convince her with his charm and charisma. They agree to get married, and Angelica finds herself caught up in a whirlwind of luxury, romance, and passion. However, as she gets to know Marco better, she realizes that there is a darker side to him and his family business, and she begins to question her decision to marry him.

chapter 2

Rafael greeted his grandmother and the family solicitor, Mr. Anderson, in the study. He didn't know why they were there, but he had a feeling it was something to do with the trust his grandfather had left him. His grandfather's will had strict instructions that he wasn't to be told what was in the trust until he turned thirty-five. Rafael still had six years to go before he reached that age. His grandmother, however, had always been very fond of him and had a tendency to meddle in his life. Rafael couldn't help wondering if this meeting had something to do with her. "Rafael, my dear boy," his grandmother said, giving him a hug. "I'm so glad you could come." Rafael pulled away, frowning. "Of course, Grandmother. But why all the secrecy? What's going on?" "Mr. Anderson has some documents he needs you to sign," his grandmother said, gesturing to the solicitor. "Documents?" Rafael repeated, feeling a sense of unease. "What kind of documents?" "Nothing to worry about," Mr. Anderson said, handing him a stack of papers. "It's just some routine paperwork. Your grandmother is concerned about your financial future, and this will help ensure your security." Rafael frowned, looking over the papers. They seemed harmless enough, but he couldn't shake the feeling that something was off. Still, he signed them, not wanting to upset his grandmother. "Is there anything else?" he asked, handing the papers back to Mr. Anderson. "Just one more thing," his grandmother said, taking a deep breath. "I've arranged a marriage for you, Rafael." Rafael felt as though he had been punched in the gut. "A what?" "A marriage," his grandmother repeated. "To a very lovely young woman. She comes from a good family, and I've made sure that the prenuptial agreement is in your favor." "A prenuptial agreement?" Rafael repeated, feeling numb. "Yes, of course," his grandmother said, patting his hand. "I wouldn't want you to be taken advantage of, my dear." Rafael stared at her, feeling as though he was in a dream. "Who is she?" he asked finally. "Her name is Isobel," his grandmother said. "Isobel Carmichael. She's the daughter of an old friend of mine." "I don't understand," Rafael said, shaking his head. "Why would you do this? I don't want to get married. And even if I did, I would want to choose my own wife." "I know, I know," his grandmother said, looking pained. "But I'm getting older, Rafael, and I worry about you. You need someone to take care of you, to love you. Isobel is a good girl. She'll make you a wonderful wife." Rafael felt sick to his stomach. He couldn't believe this was happening. "I'm sorry, Grandmother," he said, standing up. "But I can't do this. I won't." "Rafael, please," his grandmother said, standing up as well. "Don't be difficult. Think about your future." "I am thinking about my future," Rafael said, his voice rising. "And I won't be forced into a marriage I don't want. I won't let you or anyone else dictate my life." With that, he stormed out of the room, leaving his grandmother and the solicitor behind. He didn't know what he was going to do, but he knew one thing for sure. He wouldn't marry Isobel Carmichael or anyone else for that matter.

Maddie felt a shiver run through her at his words, and she couldn't help but be tempted by the idea of being with him. But she knew that there were complications to their situation, and she didn't want to make things more difficult. "I don't know if that's a good idea, Rafe," she said hesitantly. "We're still married, after all. What if we grow to care for each other, only to have to part ways in a few months' time?" Rafe reached out and took her hand, bringing it to his lips. "Then we'll make the most of the time we have, Maddie. We'll make it unforgettable." But for now, she was content to be in Rafe's arms, and she knew that whatever happened, she would always treasure the memories of their time together.
