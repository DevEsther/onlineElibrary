chapter 1

Beezus is a responsible and sensible 9-year-old girl who finds it difficult to understand her 4-year-old sister's behavior. She doesn't like how Ramona always wants to be the center of attention and never seems to take anything seriously. Beezus is especially frustrated with Ramona's habit of interrupting her whenever she is trying to read, study or play with her friends. Despite Beezus' frustration, Ramona is a loving and imaginative little girl who wants nothing more than to have fun and be loved by her family. She has a vivid imagination and a quirky personality that often get her into trouble, but she never means to cause harm or trouble.

chapter 2

Beezus and Ramona's mother is a working mom, and she enrolls Ramona in a morning nursery school program to help keep her busy and out of trouble. Beezus, who attends elementary school, takes on the role of walking Ramona to school each day. At first, Beezus is excited about the prospect of being a responsible older sister and looking out for Ramona. However, her excitement quickly fades when she realizes that walking Ramona to school is no easy task. Ramona is easily distracted and often wanders off the path, picking flowers or chasing after a dog. One day, Ramona's teacher suggests that Ramona would benefit from having her own set of keys to play with during class. Ramona takes the suggestion a bit too far and starts using the keys to open and close every door in the house. Beezus becomes irritated with the noise and disruption caused by the constant clanging of keys.

chapter 3

eezus's mother suggests that she and Ramona spend a day baking cookies together. Beezus is excited about the opportunity to bond with her sister and teach her some new skills in the kitchen. However, the baking project quickly becomes a disaster when Ramona starts eating the dough and making a mess with the flour. Beezus becomes frustrated and wonders if Ramona will ever be able to behave like a normal child.

chapter 4

Beezus's father comes home from work with a surprise for Ramona - a set of blocks to play with. Beezus is hesitant about the gift, knowing that Ramona can be destructive and might end up breaking the blocks. However, to her surprise, Ramona takes a keen interest in building with the blocks and spends hours constructing elaborate towers and bridges. Beezus realizes that she had been underestimating Ramona's capabilities and that maybe she should give her sister more credit. She starts to see Ramona in a new light and appreciates the unique qualities that make her sister special.

chapter 5

Beezus and Ramona's mother takes them to a department store to shop for a new dress for Beezus. Ramona becomes restless and starts to misbehave, throwing tantrums and running around the store. Beezus becomes embarrassed and wishes she could disappear. However, when they get home, Ramona surprises her by drawing a picture of her sister in the dress she picked out. Beezus realizes that, despite her sister's unpredictable behavior, Ramona loves and admires her in her own way.

chapter 6

Beezus is preparing to go to a party with her friend Mary. Ramona becomes upset when she realizes she's not invited to the party and begins to cry. Beezus becomes frustrated and tells Ramona that she can't always be included in everything. Ramona's tears turn into a full-blown tantrum, and Beezus feels guilty for causing her sister so much distress.
