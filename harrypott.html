chapter 1

One night, Harry is working on his homework when he notices a large black dog staring at him through the window. Harry is terrified because he believes that the dog is a death omen. The next day, Harry learns from the Daily Prophet, a wizarding newspaper, that Sirius Black, a dangerous wizard, has escaped from the wizarding prison, Azkaban. The Ministry of Magic believes that Black is after Harry. At the start of term, Harry boards the Hogwarts Express and is reunited with his best friends, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger. On the train, they meet a new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, Professor Lupin, who quickly becomes Harry's favorite teacher. However, when the train stops unexpectedly, the trio is confronted by Dementors, Azkaban's guards, who are searching for Sirius Black. The Dementors cause Harry to relive his parents' murder, and he passes out. When he wakes up, Professor Lupin is giving him chocolate, which helps to alleviate the effects of the Dementors' presence. Later, Professor Lupin teaches Harry how to perform the Patronus Charm, a powerful spell that can ward off Dementors. As the school year progresses, Harry becomes convinced that Sirius Black is after him, and he and his friends try to uncover the truth. They learn that Black was a close friend of Harry's parents and was falsely accused of betraying them to Voldemort. In the end, it is revealed that the real betrayer was Peter Pettigrew, who has been posing as Ron's pet rat, Scabbers. The book ends with a dramatic confrontation between Harry, Ron, Hermione, and Sirius Black, in which Pettigrew is revealed to be alive and Sirius is exonerated of all charges. The trio also discovers that Professor Lupin is a werewolf and has been helping Sirius, but they keep his secret and he is allowed to keep his job at Hogwarts. Harry leaves Hogwarts with a new understanding of his parents' past and a sense of hope for the future.

chapter 2

As Harry wanders the streets, he spots a large black dog that seems to be following him. The dog is unnerving, but Harry dismisses it as coincidence and continues on his way. However, as he approaches a dark alleyway, the dog suddenly attacks him. Harry is able to defend himself using a spell he learned at Hogwarts, but he is left shaken and bewildered. After the attack, Harry is approached by a group of wizards who were patrolling the area. They introduce themselves as members of the Order of the Phoenix, a secret society of wizards and witches who work to protect the magical world from dark forces. The Order's leader, Albus Dumbledore, has tasked them with keeping an eye on Harry after he escaped from the Dursley's. The wizards explain to Harry that he is in grave danger because the prisoner who escaped from Azkaban, notorious wizard Sirius Black, is believed to be after him. They reveal that Sirius is a convicted murderer and that he was once a close friend of Harry's parents. The group takes Harry to a safe house where he can hide until they can figure out how to keep him safe. As Harry settles into the safe house, he can't shake the feeling that he's being watched. He also has vivid dreams about flying on a broomstick and seeing a mysterious figure in the distance. Despite his fear and confusion, Harry finds comfort in the company of his friends Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, who are staying at the safe house with him.

chapter 3

As Harry wanders the streets, he sees a giant, triple-decker, violently purple bus approaching him. On the bus, Harry meets an elderly witch named Irma Pince who gives him a reassuring smile and a free hot chocolate. He also encounters a mysterious, cloaked figure who is wrapped in bandages and seems to be in a great deal of pain. The figure is later revealed to be Sirius Black, the notorious prisoner who has escaped from Azkaban. The bus takes Harry to the Leaky Cauldron in Diagon Alley, where he is greeted by the friendly innkeeper, Tom. Harry overhears a conversation between Tom and Cornelius Fudge, the Minister of Magic, who has arrived to investigate the escape of Sirius Black. Fudge informs Tom that Black is after Harry and that he must be kept safe at all costs. As Harry is ushered away to a private room, he hears Fudge and Tom discussing the fact that Hogwarts will be the safest place for Harry to stay, and that he must be accompanied by a Dementor, a creature known for sucking the happiness out of people. The chapter ends with Harry pondering the fact that he is not safe, even in the wizarding world, and that he is in grave danger from an unknown enemy.

chapter 4

After overhearing the conversation between Minister Fudge and Tom, the innkeeper of the Leaky Cauldron, Harry realizes that he is in danger and decides to take matters into his own hands. He sets out to find out more about Sirius Black and why he might be after him. Harry visits Flourish and Blotts, a bookstore in Diagon Alley, and finds a book about the history of Azkaban, the wizarding prison from which Black has escaped. He learns about the inhumane treatment of prisoners and the use of Dementors as guards, which only confirms his fear of having to be in close proximity to one at Hogwarts. Later, Harry runs into Hermione and Ron, who have also arrived in Diagon Alley. They have been working on their summer assignments and are excited to see Harry. They catch up and Harry tells them about his encounter with the Knight Bus and the ominous news about Black. After some time in Diagon Alley, Harry, Ron, and Hermione head back to the Hogwarts Express to begin their journey to Hogwarts. On the train, they are joined by a new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, Professor Lupin, who Harry immediately takes a liking to. Lupin teaches Harry a powerful spell called "Expecto Patronum," which can repel Dementors. As the train approaches Hogwarts, the students are told to put on their robes and prepare for the arrival at Hogsmeade Station. Harry, Ron, and Hermione excitedly make their way to the front of the train to catch a glimpse of Hogwarts Castle, eager to begin another year of magical adventures.

chapter 5

As Harry, Ron, and Hermione make their way to the Hogwarts Castle, they see the black dog again, and Harry becomes convinced that it is following him. They arrive at Hogwarts and are sorted into their respective houses. Harry is pleased to be back in Gryffindor with his friends. The next day, Harry attends his first Defense Against the Dark Arts class with Professor Lupin. He is impressed with Lupin's teaching methods and feels that he is finally learning useful spells to defend himself against dark magic. However, during the lesson, Harry is asked to perform the "Expecto Patronum" spell to ward off a Boggart, a shape-shifting creature that takes the form of the person's worst fear. Harry's worst fear is a Dementor, and when he tries to cast the spell, he is overwhelmed by the presence of one. After the class, Harry confides in Lupin about his experience with the Dementor. Lupin explains that Dementors are creatures that feed on human happiness and that their presence can cause depression, despair, and even death. He also reveals that they have been stationed around Hogwarts to guard against Sirius Black, who is believed to be after Harry. Over the next few weeks, Harry is haunted by the presence of the Dementors. He becomes increasingly afraid and withdrawn, and his academic performance suffers. He also experiences vivid flashbacks of his parents' murder, which he believes are triggered by the Dementors' presence. One night, while returning from a visit to Hagrid's hut, Harry is attacked by a group of Dementors. He tries to cast the "Expecto Patronum" spell, but it fails, and he faints. He is rescued by a mysterious figure who casts a powerful Patronus, driving the Dementors away.
